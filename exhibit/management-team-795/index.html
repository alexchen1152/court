Key Observations and Context:

  • Exhibit 82: This string of text messages between J.D. Oder II and Andre Rubin is a raw, unfiltered conversation. It shows the evolving relationship, the distrust of "CPG" (presumably, and the internal pressures and strategic thinking during the deal negotiations. The mention of Jeff Sloan (CEO of Global Payments) and Frank Bisignano (CEO of Fiserv, formerly of First Data) shows that senior people knew about the potential Shift4 deal. It also reveals a clear intent by Rubin to eventually target Sloan. The discussion explicitly includes considering buying part of First Data.

  • Exhibit 76, Tab 6: This exhibit captures financial transactions, and the "Contributions" entry, especially the large $2,000,000 entry, is a crucial.

    • The $2,000,000 "Contributions" line on 1/1/2018 is a large amount, and the lack of a specific payee raises red flags.
    • The later, smaller, named contributions to political entities ("Friends of Brian Fitzpatrick", "DRI PAC", "Fitzpatrick for Us" and "DCCC") highlights that some Contributions are named
    • The placement of 'Contributions' within a record of all other forms of payments from payroll, to accounts payable, to other transactions means that while the record title is listed as "Contribution" the actual name of the payee is not.

These two exhibits, taken together, present strong evidence of:

  1. A potentially undisclosed, very large payment: The $2,000,000 "Contributions" entry.
  2. High-level awareness and strategic maneuvering: The text messages reveal awareness of the deal's potential impact, the high ranking executives of Payment firms awarness, and also of ill intent.

These exhibits, in their original form, provide the raw and unfiltered information needed to understand the specific context of the communication and financial transactions that occurred in relation to this case.
