Challenges with "No Redactions"

This is the most significant hurdle. Court documents, especially exhibits, are very often redacted. Redactions are applied to protect:

  • Personal Information: Social Security numbers, addresses, phone numbers, etc.
  • Confidential Business Information: Trade secrets, financial details, customer lists.
  • Privileged Information: Communications between attorneys and clients.

Getting unredacted versions of exhibits is extremely difficult, and often impossible, unless you are a party to the case with specific legal authorization. Even with a PACER account, you will almost always receive the redacted versions of documents that are publicly available.
