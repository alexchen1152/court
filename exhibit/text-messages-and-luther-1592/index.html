Summary of Findings and Key Takeaways

  • Authenticity Confirmed: The deposition of J.J. Luther (Document 260-12) explicitly confirms the authenticity of the text message exchanges presented in Exhibits E and P (Documents 230-5, 230-6, and 236-16). He acknowledges sending and receiving the messages.
  • Family Emergency: Luther references a significant family emergency (his mother's immaterial attempt) as a reason for delays in communication. This is present in text, and confirmed in his deposition.
  • Funding Issues: A recurring theme is Luther's concern about merchants being "double billed" and funds being held by Card Connect, causing difficulties for his clients. He explains this in detail during his deposition.
  • Requests for Information: Taylor (from Card Connect) urgently requests information from Luther regarding specific transactions, and Luther explains his delays in providing it.
  • Processing Platforms: Luther mentions using multiple payment processing platforms (cybersource, authorize.net, Vantiv, First Data, TSYS, Elavon) and clarifies that his company is a national ISO, not Card Connect.
  • Redactions: Significant redactions exist, obscuring the identities of some individuals involved in the conversations and potentially some financial details. However the context from around, combined with deposition gives some clues.
