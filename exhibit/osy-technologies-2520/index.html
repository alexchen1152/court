economic structures here, but the bottom line is OSY has been a customer
of Card Connect for years and processes billions. They are a profitable
account for Card Connect (and us a little bit) and they are at risk of leaving.
We need to fix that.

(4) Jointly develop a plan to move forward with respect to at-risk customers
representing over $7B in payment volume. These include: OSY, Shopkeep,
Lavu, MobileBytes, Simpay, POS Nation, etc. I acknowledge that every

customers by the end of next week to show a united front and that
FD/Card Connect is committed to getting their problems solved.

believe the pricing of SkyTab is being unfairly marked up a lot to other
ISOs/competitors... despite the fact that (i) Card Connect imposed
exclusivity of SkyTab for those ISOs and (ii) we developed SkyTab.

(6) Resolve the PAX hardware inventory issue. There must be 5,000+ devices
in that King of Prussia warehouse that we paid for that have never been
deployed.

(7) Agree to terms that protect our customers from solicitation/conversion -
especially by Card Connect direct/Fiserv direct.

(8) Make changes to the referral agreement that reflect the termination
provisions of the operating agreement and improve some other areas
discovered throughout this battle/litigation.

I appreciate the above represents a lot of work. Again, I do think we
have a lot of common ground through which an agreement can be
reached that is mutually beneficial for both parties. I personally, as
well as many others on my team, have dedicated ourselves day and
night to achieve this outcome and look forward to hearing from you to
get it done.

Best,
Jared
