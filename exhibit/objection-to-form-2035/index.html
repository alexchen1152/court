Key Findings and Observations:

  • Exhibit 39 (Docket 201): This provides a very candid, real-time glimpse into Shift4's internal discussions and opinions about First Data (Fiserv) and Deanna Johnson during a critical board meeting. The participants express strong negative opinions and use derogatory language. This suggests a very strained and adversarial relationship.
  • Exhibit 5 (Docket 253): The messages are a discussion between Taylor Lauber and Daniel Roberts expressing concerns and frustration about the contract and the impact that it will have on operations. This reinforces the difficulties and tensions between the two sides.
  • The "objection to form" designation on the deposition transcripts (Dockets 223 and 260) commonly means that the opposing counsel is preserving their right to object to the way a question was asked during the deposition, not necessarily the content itself. It's a procedural objection that doesn't impact the availability of the content.
  • I did not find any more exhibits that showed text messages.

This complete presentation of the relevant exhibits provides valuable insight into the breakdown of the relationship.
