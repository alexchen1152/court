Case Background:

This case was a contract dispute between Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments, LLC. Card Connect alleged that Shift4 breached their contract and engaged in unfair competition. The exhibits were submitted as evidence to support the claims and defenses of both parties.

Key Observations and Important Note:

  • Secrecy and "Going Dark": The text messages clearly indicate an attempt to keep information about Shift4's actions from Card Connect. Phrases like "going dark," "ghost," and "way too risky" strongly suggest a deliberate effort to conceal their activities.
  • Paintball as a Cover: The mention of paintball appears to be used as a potential alibi or cover story. Taylor Laabs instructs someone to say they were at paintball with him and another individual if questioned.
  • Scrubbing Social Media: Jared Isaacman's directive to "scrub" social media posts related to the Lighthouse upgrade program further reinforces the intent to hide their actions from Card Connect. This indicates an awareness that the posts could reveal their strategy.
  • JRR was not apart of those text messages.
  • Contract: The ISO agreement sets the foundation for the relationship and duties, which are now in dispute, within this document.
