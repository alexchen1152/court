Key Observations and Context

  • Pressure for Revenue Recognition: The emails and depositions clearly indicate substantial pressure from Jared Isaacman and accounting to recognize revenue quickly, even influencing the legal drafting and execution process.
  • Timeline Issues: The emails show back-and-forth regarding the effective date and signature of the CardConnect/Bridge agreement, with concerns raised about meeting audit requirements.
  • Lauber's Role: Taylor Lauber, as legal counsel, was caught between the pressure to recognize revenue and ensuring legal compliance. Her deposition confirms she acted on Isaacman's instructions.
  • Stio's Involvement: J. Angelo Stio, as Chief Legal Officer, was also involved in the negotiations and felt the pressure to finalize the agreement for revenue recognition purposes.
  • Backdating was a key topic to ensure the effective date of May 1, 2017 was realized.

The exhibits, taken together, show a focus on using contractual language to reach a very specific financial goal.
