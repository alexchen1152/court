From: Rock Jones
Sent: Monday, June 08, 2020 12:52 PM

Subject:Sky Chiefs

Sky Chiefs

In 2016 Shift4 began a relationship with the company that supplies all the air to air refueling for the military, Sky Chiefs.
This included all the C17, KC135 and other military aircraft with fuel services, They were originally going to use Pivotal
for payment processing, but Pivotal could not pre-authorize the way that Shift4 could. Since many transactions are in
the air and approved after landing, Shift4 can hold pre-auths for over 30 days vs the industry standard of 7 days. The
average fuel ticket is $300,000,
They moved their entire business to Shift4 based on that pre-auth ability(they call it a hold), Because they did not bill for
days and often wecks after fueling, they wanted a POS system that could pre-auth, hold and then bill for full amount or
less, but never for more. They became a big customer for us.
On February, I got a message from Global, the processor for Sky Chiefs saying that we owed Global $100,000+
because we were not able to hold these authorizations and our system was doing something called a "force post"
This was basically voiding the original authorization, and doing another transaction for the correct amount. That is all
fine, but ifthe account turns out to be stolen, closed or bad account, they have to pay a much higher fee, plus the amount
of the transaction, The total amount in question was millions of dollars.
I immediately went to JD and Randy with the situation. Because of my friend owning Sky Chiefs, I had a few heated
discussions on the importance of fixing this. Shift4 kept talking to them and assuring them it was fixed, only to have a
few force posts a weck later. They were livid,
It's important to note, at this point, that only Shift4 can sell Shift4 and Sky Chiefs could only stay with us if we allowed
Global to maintain the processing relationship. I told the owner, Dave, thal I would personally have to go back 10 Shift4
with my tail between my legs. I had to go back and convince JD to allow Global be the reseller and sell Shift4,
Because I was the one who brought Sky Chiefs to Shift4, I was embarrassed that it took months to get this issue
resolved for them, This was not a good reflection on me. I believe this is another cxample of me trying to go the extra
mile for my personal contacts.

From: Taylor Lauber [tlauber@shift4.com]
Sent: Friday, March 27, 2020 3:49 PM
To: 'Jeff Shanahan' [jshanahan@cardconnect.com]
Cc: Legal [legal@shift4.com]; 'Mike Sommers [msomemrs@fiserv.com]'
Subject: Contractual Dispute

Jeff,

I am writing to you regarding our Reseller Agreement, dated March
27, 2014 and all amendments thereto (the "Agreement"). We have valued
our relationship with CardConnect; which has stretched well over a
decade. However, circumstances have changed and necessitate the
termination of our agreement. Specifically, the nature of the "control"
of CardConnect has changed, and as a result of this change in
control, Section 11.4 of the Agreement permits Lighthouse (Shift4) to
terminate the Agreement.

Section 11.4 of the Agreement provides in relevant part (with
emphasis added):
    This Agreement shall terminate, at the non-breaching
    Party's option....upon a change in control of the other
    Party. A change in control for purposes hereof shall
    include the acquisition of more than fifty percent (50%) of
    the outstanding equity of a Party...
    Upon a change in control of CardConnect, Lighthouse, may
    in its sole discretion, terminate this agreement…

Pursuant to the above-mentioned provisiosn, Lighthouse is exercising its right to
terminate the Agreement due to a change in control of CardConnect.
Namely, CardConnect merged with, and was subsequently acquired by,
First Data in 2017. Lighthouse did not exercise its right under this
change in control provision at that time. However, in 2019, First
Data was acquired by Fiserv. It goes without saying that Fiserv is a
competitor of Lighthouse, and in fact currently, is one of our most
aggressive competitors. As a result of these acquisitions and
combinations, "control" of CardConnect now rests in the hands of
Fiserv. Accordingly, Lighthouse now gives its formal notice of
termination, immediately and without further notice.

While the Agreement is clear on the effect of the change in control
provisions cited above, it does lack clarity regarding our financial
obligations following temination. As such, despite its termination,
Lighthouse believes that it has an obligation to effect a settlement of
all amounts due CardConnect under the Agreement. Accordingly, we
look forward to your calculations as to any financial amounts due or
owing.

We thank you.
Talyor Lauber.
