Final Summary:

The exhibits, most notably the text message exchanges between J.D. Oder, II and Jared Isaacman (Exhibits O, P, and Q), and the deposition excerpts (Exhibits C, D, N and W) are the most telling. They show:

  • Initial Concern: A Shift4 sales representative (Rob McGovern) encountered a prospect using CardConnect, leading to an inquiry about CardConnect's status (Exhibit O, and D).
  • Blocking Capability: Jared Isaacman stated he had "complete control" of the gateway and could block CardConnect (Exhibit O). He later affirms that blocking was done beyond the reseller ID level (Exhibit N).
  • Reseller Concerns: J.D. Oder, II raised concerns about the large number of CardConnect resellers (1,800+) and the potential impact of blocking CardConnect on relationships with those resellers, some of whom Shift4 might be working with through Taylor Laemmle (Exhibit P, Exhibit W).
  • Taylor Laemmle's Role: Taylor Laemmle was tasked with building out a third-party distribution team and was, at least initially, supposed to partner with all resellers, including CardConnect agents (Exhibit C). His deposition indicates some ambiguity in the instructions he received, and confirmation he was still contacting them.
  • Isaacman uncertainty: admitted he was apart of the decision to block, but did no know if his own "finger pushed the key"(Exhibit N)
  • Oder Knowledge: was aware of the sunset, and concerned of the many partners and resources issue, but authorized no action. (Exhibit W)

The documents reveal a clear internal discussion at Shift4 about blocking CardConnect, confirmation that Shift4 could block CardConnect, concerns about the impact of such a block, and ultimately, confirmation that a block did occur, although the exact extent and instigator of the block are less precise within these specific documents. The deposition testimonies confirm these discussions and concerns exist. Importantly, Isaacmans direct admission is clear.
