KEY TAKEAWAYS from the communications:

  • Intent: The conversations between Jared Isaacman (jisaacman) and Taylor Isaacman (tisaacman) clearly show an intent to leverage Shift4's control over the 2FA implementation to negatively impact CardConnect. The "massacre" and "Pearl Harbor" analogies, along with discussions about CardConnect's market cap and EBITDA, are very strong evidence.
  • Targeting: They discuss identifying and potentially exempting certain merchant categories (lodging and car rental) to mitigate the damage, but it's clear the overall goal is to convert a significant portion of CardConnect's business.
  • Coordination. They are coordinating the timing and technical implementation
  • Awareness of Impact: Jared Isaacman expresses some reluctance, acknowledging the potential harm to CardConnect, but ultimately proceeds with the plan. This is crucial because it goes directly to the question of intent.
  • Email Corrdination: Taylor Issacman wanted to get "the right email" for the tokens, they were ahead of Jared in this.

The provided exhibits strongly suggest a deliberate strategy by Shift4 to use its technical capabilities to disadvantage CardConnect, and that is main substance of the entire case.
