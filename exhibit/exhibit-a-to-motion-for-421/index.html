Accessing Court Records and Exhibits (PACER)

Federal court records in the United States, including exhibits, are primarily accessed through PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records). Here's the process and the challenges:

  1. PACER Account: You'll need a PACER account. Registration is free, but there are fees for accessing documents (usually per page).

  2. Finding the Case: Once logged into PACER, you can search for the case using the case number (1:19-cv-01718-RGA) and the court (D.Del.).

  3. Docket Sheet: The docket sheet is a chronological list of all filings in the case. This is where you'll find entries for exhibits. Exhibits are typically filed as attachments to motions, briefs, or other documents.

  4. Identifying Relevant Exhibits: You'll need to carefully review the docket entries to find those that might contain text messages from 2023. This can be time-consuming, as the descriptions are often brief (e.g., "Exhibit A to Motion for Summary Judgment").   5. Downloading and Paying: Once you identify a potentially relevant exhibit, you can download it through PACER. You'll be charged per page.

  6. Review case documents: Looking for the relevant attachments.
