Summary of Findings and Context

  • ISS World Asia Conference: The conference in Hong Kong is a recurring point of contention. Jared Isaacman (Shift4) and Peter Brickman (Card Connect) both acknowledge attending and meeting there. However, Isaacman claims no recollection of discussing or exchanging a revised ISO agreement at the conference itself, while Brickman's declaration states that Isaacman mentioned sending an updated version via voicemail.
  • Text Messages: Text messages between Brickman and Isaacman at the ISS conference, are brief exchanges about their travel schedules. Text Message exhibits between Ryan McCurry and an individual only noted as a + and a phone number discuss setting up a meeting.
  • Email Chains: An email from Isaacman to Brickman, dated October 3, 2018, attaches a document labeled "Updated ISO Agreement." A later email from Brickman to Isaacman (November 15, 2018) thanks Isaacman for sending the agreement.
  • Disagreement of the updated contract:. Jared Issacman states he sent an undated ISO to CardConnect. While Peter Brickman states CardConnect did not agree on all of the material terms.
  • Voicemail: Peter Brickman reports receiving a voicemail from Jared Isaacman at the ISS conference stating that he had sent an additional, updated copy of the ISO Agreement. Brickman states that he has no record of receiving it.
  • Deposition Testimony (DEX 31): Isaacman's deposition confirms searches for text messages between himself and Fiserv personnel, but he doesn't recall the results. He also does not recall meeting with Fiserv at ISS World.

The core issue is whether a revised agreement was effectively communicated and agreed upon. Shift4 (Isaacman) argues that a revised version was sent, while Card Connect/Fiserv (Brickman) acknowledges receipt of a version but contends that key terms were not finalized and that a further updated version supposedly sent at ISS World was never received. The text messages, while confirming contact at the conference, do not provide conclusive evidence about the agreement itself, only providing evidence about them attending the conference. The voicemail claim by Brickman is a key point of dispute.
