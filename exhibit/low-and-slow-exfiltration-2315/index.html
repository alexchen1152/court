Summary of Findings and Context

  • Exhibit 703 & 704 (Docket 253-8): These show text messages and emails. The key takeaway is the forwarding of the "CardConnect Gateway - Partner Profitability - First Draft.xlsx" file from Michael Allen (of CardConnect) to Jared Isaacman's Shift4 email address, and then Jared forwarding that to Taylor Lauber after Jared's resignation email to CardConnect.
  • Low and Slow Exfiltration (222-111): Contains the report from shift4, which flagged the aforementioned email.

The core issue highlighted by these exhibits is the transfer of a potentially sensitive/proprietary document ("Partner Profitability") from CardConnect to Shift4 employees (including Jared Isaacman, who was transitioning between the companies) very shortly after Isaacman's resignation from CardConnect. This raises concerns about the appropriateness of sharing this information, given Isaacman's move to a competitor. The "low and slow exfiltration" refers to the pattern of sending potentially sensitive data over a period, possibly to avoid detection.
