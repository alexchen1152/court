Case Background and Docket Search

The case, Card Connect, LLC v. Shift4 Payments, LLC, was filed in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania. The case number is 2:17-cv-04220. I can use this information to search dockets and related resources.

My initial search reveals the following general information and a summary of the key issues:

  • Nature of the Suit: The case involves a contract dispute between Card Connect (which was later acquired by Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments. The core issue was a "Reseller Agreement" where Card Connect resold Shift4's payment gateway services. Disagreements arose regarding exclusivity, pricing, and alleged breaches of contract. There were accusations of improper solicitation of customers, failure to meet minimum commitments, and disputes over the definition of "Transaction Fees."
  • Key Players:
    • Card Connect, LLC: The plaintiff, later acquired by Fiserv. A payment processing company.
    • Shift4 Payments, LLC: The defendant. A payment gateway and technology provider.
    • Jared Isaacman: CEO of Shift4. A significant figure named in many documents.
    • Jeff Shanahan: was Executive at Card Connect.
  • Key Claims:
    • Breach of Contract.
    • Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing.
    • Declaratory Judgment.
    • Tortious Interference with Contractual Relations (Shift4's counterclaim).
    • Misappropriation of Trade Secrets.
  • Procedural History: The case involved extensive discovery, multiple motions (including motions to dismiss, motions for summary judgment), a settlement conference, and ultimately ended with Settlement.
