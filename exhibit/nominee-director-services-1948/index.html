Case Background (for context):

  • Case Number: 1:20-cv-01198-RGA (District of Delaware)
  • Parties:
    • Plaintiff: Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv)
    • Defendant: Shift4 Payments, LLC (and later, Shift4 Payments, Inc., as it went public)
  • Nature of the Dispute: Breach of contract related to a Merchant Portfolio Purchase Agreement and, crucially, a "Nominee Director Services Agreement." Card Connect alleged Shift4 breached the agreements, particularly regarding the appointment of a nominee director to Shift4's board and the handling of certain merchant accounts.

NOMINEE DIRECTOR SERVICES AGREEMENT

This NOMINEE DIRECTOR SERVICES AGREEMENT (this “Agreement”), dated as of March  29, 2017 (the “Effective Date”), is made by and between CardConnect, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (“CardConnect”), and Shift4 Payments, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”).

RECITALS

WHEREAS, CardConnect and the Company are parties to that certain Merchant Portfolio Purchase Agreement, of even date herewith (the “Purchase Agreement”); and

WHEREAS,  in  connection  with  the  Purchase  Agreement,  CardConnect  desires  to  appoint  a representative to serve as a non-voting observer to the board of managers of the Company (the “Board of Managers”), and, at the Company’s request if ever the Company converts to a corporation, a non-voting observer to the board of directors of the Company (the “Board of Directors” and, together with the Board of Managers or any other governing body of the Company, the “Board”), and the Company is willing to permit CardConnect to appoint such representative, in accordance with the terms and conditions set forth herein.

NOW,  THEREFORE,  in  consideration  of  the  mutual  covenants  and  agreements  set forth  herein  and  for  good  and  valuable  consideration,  the  receipt  and  sufficiency  of  which  are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. Appointment of Observer.

(a) Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, so long as CardConnect or any of its Affiliates, collectively, own at least five percent (5%) of the outstanding equity interests in the Company on a fully-diluted basis (the “Minimum Ownership Threshold”), CardConnect shall, as the holder of this right, have the right to appoint one individual to serve as a non-voting observer to the Board (the “Observer”).  The initial Observer shall be Jeffrey Shanahan or, if Jeffrey Shanahan  is  unable  or  unwilling  to  serve,  such  other  person  as  mutually  agreed  in  writing between CardConnect and the Company, and any replacement Observer must be mutually agreed in writing between CardConnect and the Company.
(b) CardConnect may remove its Observer at any time and for any reason or no reason by providing written notice thereof to the Company. In the event that an Observer appointed by CardConnect shall cease to serve as Observer for any reason, CardConnect shall, as the holder of this right, have the right to appoint a replacement Observer so long as it meets the Minimum Ownership  Threshold.
(c) The Company shall invite the Observer to attend all meetings of the Board (and any committees thereof) in a nonvoting observer capacity and, in this respect, shall give such
Observer copies of all notices, minutes, consents, and other materials that it provides to its directors or managers (as applicable) at the same time and in the same manner as provided to such directors or managers (as applicable); provided, however, that the Observer shall agree to
hold  in  confidence  and  trust  and  act  in  a  fiduciary  manner  with  respect  to  all  information
provided to him or her, and shall not disclose to any third-party (other than to CardConnect and its Affiliates, advisors, and representatives) any confidential information, including, without limitation,  any  information  regarding  the  Company  or  this  Agreement.;  provided,  further,
however, the Company reserves the right to exclude such Observer from access to any material or meeting  or  portion  thereof  if  the  Company,  upon  advice  of  counsel,  determines  that  such exclusion  is  reasonably  necessary  to  preserve  attorney-client  privilege,  to  protect  highly confidential proprietary information (including with respect to pricing, business strategy and trade secrets), avoid a conflict of interest, for other similar good-faith business reasons, of [sic] if CardConnect or its Affiliates, including its Observer (i) is a competitor or potential competitor, (ii) is in  breach  of  its  obligations  under  the  Purchase  Agreement  or  any  Related  Agreement  (as
defined in the Purchase Agreement).

2. Reimbursement of Expenses.  The Company will reimburse the Observer for any reasonable documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with his or her attendance at Board or committee meetings.

3.  Term. This Agreement shall remain in effect until the earliest of (a) the date on which CardConnect no longer meets the Minimum Ownership Threshold, (b) a Deemed Liquidation Event (as defined in the Company’s limited liability company agreement, as it may be amended, modified or restated from time to time) and (c) the termination of this Agreement by mutual written consent of CardConnect and the Company.

4.  Miscellaneous.

(a) Successors and Assigns.  This Agreement shall inure to the benefit of and be binding upon each of the parties hereto and their respective successors and permitted assigns. Neither  this  Agreement  nor  any  of  the  rights,  interests  or  obligations  hereunder  may  be  assigned by any party hereto without the prior written consent of the other party hereto, except CardConnect may, without consent of the Company, assign this Agreement to its successor-in-interest in connection with a sale of all or substantially all of its assets.
(b) Amendment and Modification. This Agreement may not be amended, modified or  supplemented  in  any  manner,  whether  by  course  of  conduct  or  otherwise,  except  by  an instrument in writing specifically designated as an amendment hereto, signed on behalf of each party.
(c) Waiver.  No failure or delay on the part of any party hereto in the exercise of any right hereunder shall impair such right or be construed to be a waiver of, or acquiescence in, any  breach  of  any  representation,  warranty  or  agreement  herein,  nor  shall  any  single  or  partial exercise of any such right preclude other or further exercise thereof or of any other right.
