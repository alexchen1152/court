Summary and Key Takeaways from the Documents:

  • Direct Admission of "Fake Traffic": The August 8, 2016 text message exchange between J. Andre (Jad) Isaac and Jared Isaacman is the most explicit: "I hear that's there's 3mm in fake traffic." "Yep." This directly references the core issue.
  • Magnitude of the Issue: The texts and the recorded conversation repeatedly highlight the $3 million figure ("3mm") and that it represented approximately 10% of Shift4's processing volume at the time.
  • Lack of Visibility and Control: Jared Isaacman consistently expresses his concern and frustration about the lack of visibility into the source and nature of this "fake traffic." He emphasizes the risk it poses to Shift4.
  • Frank L.'s Responses: In the recorded conversation, Frank L. repeatedly states that he doesn't know the source or nature of the merchants generating this traffic ("I really don't know"). This lack of knowledge is a central point of contention.
  • Intent to Deconvert: Jared Isaacman states definitively his intent to "deconvert" (terminate) the merchants responsible for this traffic within 30 days.
  • Admission of "Lying": Isaacman states that the traffic is coming from a "merchant that is lying to us". This is referenced multiple times throughout the exhibits.
  • Impact of the Issue: The exhibits provides context for their actions. The first text message thread on 7/26/16 indicates that both parties agreed that they shouldnt have kicked them off the platform from the revenue perspective.
