Challenges and Limitations:

  • PACER Access: The definitive source for U.S. Federal Court documents is PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records). Accessing PACER requires an account and involves per-page fees. I, as an AI, cannot directly access or pay for PACER documents.

  • OCR Limitations: If I find images of scanned documents, Optical Character Recognition (OCR) may be imperfect, leading to potential errors in the transcribed text.

As I mentioned, we are going to play the “Good Cop” role in this negotiation with Noble…meaning we have to give a

        *   Milestone 2 – Total monthly net revenue from combined new merchants boarded after August 1, 2018

    *  We will incorporate $100k of additional consideration for every quarter you complete the migration of the

From: Jared Isaacman
Sent: Monday, May 07, 2018 11:47 AM
To: Randy Miskanic; Taylor Lauber
Cc: Nate Hirshman; Kyle Lutteroty; Daniel K. Devine
Subject: CardConnect

All,

I had a good call with Mike Noble at Card Connect this morning. Overall, I think it went well, and we covered
a lot of ground.

Here are the key takeaways…

1.  The reseller agreement looks pretty much done. Kyle is going to make another call to Mike to close out
    any remaining items. We should assume the deal is signed this week (as a reseller) and we can begin
    referring Card Connect business shortly thereafter.
2.  I covered the overall acquisition in detail with Mike. I introduced the idea of dividing the transaction
    into multiple parts. First, the Reseller period. Second, a short exclusivity period (30-45 days) where
    we would come to terms on an overall plan to migrate all Card Connect Customers to Shift4 over a
    multi-year period. Last, implementation of the plan with an ultimate sunset to that migration period at the
    end of 2024.
3.  Mike introduced the idea of a $35M acquisition price, which I agreed seemed reasonable for the ~$6M in
    net revenue run rate of their book. I mentioned that the only way that valuation worked was if we had a
    plan in place to move all those customers to Shift4 and within a reasonable timeframe. We also agreed
    that paying incentives at the end of a 3-4 year migration period would be less desirable to both him and
    us. Mike agreed there was a lot of synergy between our organizations. He said, “We can create a lot of
    shareholder value between our two companies.”
4.  I mentioned we would not be able to keep merchants on the old Card Connect platform indefinitely. This
    didn’t seem to bother Mike. We discussed having a plan in place to convey to the agents and merchants
    that there will be great new products + incentives to move to Shift4 and for those that chose to remain
    behind, it was very likely their pricing would increase substantially – as will no longer be supporting that
    technology long term.

    **Overall, I was trying to make it clear there were two parts to this transaction. First, a short term reseller
    agreement while we completed a plan that supported a $35M acquisition price. Second, an
    implementation component, which included completing a middleware “tech to tech” integration between
    our companies. The acquisition would be contingent on the successful completion of the plan and an
    implementation that was acceptable to both organizations.

    This is important because it gives us plenty of outs if they don’t deliver what is needed from our
    technical assessment.**

5.  Mike is going to send a term sheet later this week that will include an incentive component for
    the additional revenue that is generated from integrating our technologies together. For example,
    making Harbortouch and a number of the middleware POS systems that we work with compatible
    with Card Connect should yield considerable incremental revenue. We discussed an incentive
    component tied to this additional revenue.
6.  I said we would likely pay a portion of the transaction price in cash and the remainder in stock. This
    didn’t seem to bother Mike, as he mentioned there were members of the organization that were
    already shareholders in the company.
7.  Mike also mentioned the Harbortouch-Elavon portfolio many times. I believe he is assuming that as a
    result of this overall acquisition, that we would begin migrating Harbortouch-Elavon merchants to Card
    Connect during the “implementation phase.” I didn’t commit to that in any way, other than to say
    we were working towards a single platform solution, but it was never expressly stated that we would
    move Harbortouch-Elavon merchants to Card Connect.
8.  Mike seemed very excited when I shared the capabilities within the reseller agreement to put the
    Harbortouch gateway and third-party POS systems on Card Connect. He said, “This is a game changer”
    and would come with significant cost savings.
9. Mike is going to try and connect with Frank on the gateway. He will also reach out to Sam to answer
    any remaining questions, in terms of the integration capabilities of Card Connect.
10. Mike is going to begin working with their team to establish a timeline for integrating the Shift4
    middleware to Card Connect. I mentioned we should assume we will be integrating to multiple
    processors even after such a transaction is completed. We discussed using the same format as we
    do with our existing gateway partners.
11. I did mention to Mike, that should we get to a final executable agreement to acquire the company, that we
    have to be able to get terms in there that allow us an out in case of a material adverse change in their
    business (attrition) between contract signing and closing. He did agree with that.

I am ok with the $35M provided it includes an acceptable plan to get merchants converted to
Shift4/Dollars-on-the-Net within 4 years. I think an approach, like the one mentioned above, gives us the
flexibility to make a thorough technical assessment before committing to any acquisition. I think we should
expect a Term Sheet from Mike soon. We will follow our “Good Cop/Bad Cop” approach (I’m Good Cop of
course) in order to arrive at something reasonable.

Please give me a call if you have any questions.

Thanks,

Jared
