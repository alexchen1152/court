Accessing Court Records (and the Limitations)

Court records in the U.S. federal system are generally accessible through a system called PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records). However, there are a few important caveats:

  • PACER Requires an Account and Fees: Accessing documents on PACER requires a paid account. There are fees per page viewed or downloaded.
  • Not Everything is Online: While many documents are filed electronically, some exhibits, especially large physical items or older documents, might not be available electronically. They might be described in the docket, but you'd need to contact the court clerk to arrange viewing.
  • Sealed Documents: Some documents or exhibits might be filed under seal, meaning they are not publicly accessible. This is often done to protect trade secrets, confidential business information, or personal privacy. We won't be able to access sealed documents.
  • Redaction May Exist Even Noted: Even if the entry doesn't mention redaction, redactions can occur in images or pdfs.
