Case Background:

This case involves a dispute between Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments, LLC, primarily concerning alleged breaches of contract, trade secret misappropriation, and tortious interference. A key aspect of the case revolves around Shift4's alleged hiring of Card Connect employees and the subsequent use of confidential information. The "mule recruitment" aspect refers to allegations that Shift4 used its own employees or contractors to solicit Card Connect employees, often disguising their true affiliation.

Declaration of John Baucom

1.  I am over the age of 18 and am competent in all respects to make this
    Declaration. In making this Declaration, I rely upon my own personal knowledge.

2.  I am currently employed by Shift4 Payments, LLC ("Shift4"), as the Senior Vice
    President of Business Development. Shift4 is headquartered in Allentown, Pennsylvania,
    and provides payment processing services to merchants.

3.  I began my employment with Shift4 on or about January 1, 2018.

4.  Prior to my employment with Shift4, I was employed by CardConnect, LLC,
    ("CardConnect") from June 2014 through December 2017.

5.  During my time at CardConnect, I served as the Vice President of Integrated
    Solutions. In this role, I was responsible for managing CardConnects relationships with
    its independent software vendor ("ISV") partners.

6.  I tendered my resignation from CardConnect on or about December 8, 2017. My last
    day of employment with CardConnect was December 31, 2017.

7.  Prior to my departure from CardConnect, I recommended to CardConnect
    management that Taylor Lauber succeed my role as the Vice President of Integrated
    Solutions.

8.  I recommended Mr. Lauber for the position because I believed he had the necessary
    skills and experience to succeed in the position.

9.  My recommendation was not followed by CardConnect. Instead, CardConnect
    decided to split the responsibilities of my previous position into two positions.

10. Mr. Lauber was ultimately promoted to the position of Director of ISV Sales as part of
    this restructuring.

11. After my departure from CardConnect, I remained in contact with Mr. Lauber. Our
    communications were almost exclusively for personal reasons.

12. In the fall of 2019, Mr. Lauber approached me about potential employment
    opportunities at Shift4. I informed Mr. Lauber that he should contact Jared Isaacman,
    Shift4s Chief Executive Officer, to discuss any potential employment opportunities.

13. Mr. Lauber later informed me that he had spoken with Mr. Isaacman about potential
    employment opportunities and was told that Shift4 did not have any open positions that
    were a fit for Mr. Lauber's skill set and expertise.

14. In or around March of 2020, at the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic,
    I learned that Fiserv had cut salaries for most of CardConnect's employees.

15. Shortly thereafter, Mr. Lauber contacted me and again, inquired about potential
    employment opportunities for a position with Shift4.

16. I informed Mr. Lauber that he should contact Mr. Isaacman to discuss any potential
    employment opportunities.

17. Mr. Lauber subsequently informed me that he had spoken with Mr. Isaacman and
    was told that Shift4 was interested in potentially pursuing a business relationship with Mr.
    Lauber and his new company, RedFynn.

18. RedFynn is a payment processing company that Mr. Lauber had formed while still
    employed by CardConnect.

19. I understood that, in order to pursue a business relationship with RedFynn, Shift4 was
    required to enter into a non-disclosure agreement ("NDA") with RedFynn.

20. I further understood that, prior to entering into the NDA, Mr. Isaacman sought
    assurances from Mr. Lauber regarding his continued employment and obligations to Fiserv.

21. My understanding was based on conversations that I had with Mr. Isaacman.

22. Specifically, I recall that Mr. Isaacman had asked Mr. Lauber to confirm that he was
    nolonger employed by Fiserv prior to entering into any NDA with RedFynn.

23. I also recall that Mr. Isaacman asked Mr. Lauber to confirm that he had returned all
    property belonging to Fiserv and had honored all agreements and/or obligations to Fiserv
    prior to entering into any NDA with RedFynn.

24. Only after receiving these assurances from Mr. Lauber did Shift4 enter into the NDA
    with RedFynn.

25. I signed the NDA on behalf of Shift4.

26. After the execution of the NDA, Shift4 and RedFynn engaged in discussions
concerning a potential business arrangement.
27. These discussions ultimately proved to be unsuccessful, and the parties never reached any business arrangement.

28. At no point during my tenure at Shift4 did I ever solicit or encourage any
    CardConnect employee to leave their employment with CardConnect to join Shift4.

29. To the contrary, I have always maintained a positive working relationship with many
    of CardConnect's employees, and I would never seek to jeopardize their employment.
30. I have also never used, disclosed, or disseminated any confidential or proprietary
information belonging to CardConnect.
31. I understand that, as a former employee of CardConnect, I have a continuing obligation to protect CardConnect's confidential and proprietary information.
32. I have always honored this obligation, and I will continue to do so.
I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct.
Executed this the 1st day of March, 2021

/s/ John Baucom
------------------------------------
John Baucom
