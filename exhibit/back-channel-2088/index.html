Case Background:

This case involved a contractual dispute between Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments, LLC, stemming from their reseller agreement. A key issue was the alleged breach of contract and the use of a "back-channel" by Shift4 to communicate with Card Connect/Fiserv employees.

Key Observations and Context:

  • "FB" = Frank Bisignano: The exhibits clearly show that "FB" is a reference to Frank Bisignano, who was the CEO of Fiserv at the time.
  • Taylor Lauber as Intermediary: Taylor Lauber, who worked at Fiserv, acted as a go-between for Isaacman (Shift4's CEO) and Bisignano. This is the core of the "back-channel" communication alleged in the case.
  • Timing: The dates of these communications (August - November 2020) are significant, as they coincide with the period of contractual dispute and alleged breaches.
  • Communications are requests for conversations.
