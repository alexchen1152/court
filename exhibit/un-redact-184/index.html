I. Legal Standard
The scope of permissible discovery is broad, but not unlimited. Parties are permitted to
‚Äúobtain discovery regarding any nonprivileged matter that is relevant to any party‚Äôs claim or
defense and proportional to the needs of the case . . . .‚Äù Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(b)(1). Information is
relevant, and thus discoverable, if it ‚Äúappears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of
admissible evidence.‚Äù Oppenheimer Fund, Inc. v. Sanders, 437 U.S. 340, 351-52 (1978). The
Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, including the rules governing discovery, must be ‚Äúconstrued,
administered, and employed by the court and the parties to secure the just, speedy, and inexpensive
determination of every action and proceeding.‚Äù Fed. R. Civ. P. 1. In furtherance of that purpose,
the Federal Rules permit a party to move to compel discovery responses when a party fails to respond
to proper discovery requests. Fed. R. Civ. P. 37(a)(3)(B).
