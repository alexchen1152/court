Background and Case Information

First, it's important to understand the context. This case was a lawsuit in the Delaware Court of Chancery:

  • Case Name: Card Connect, LLC v. Shift4 Payments, LLC, et al.
  • Court: Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware
  • Case Number: C.A. No. 2019-0671-JRS
  • Date Filed: August 27, 2019

The case involved a contractual dispute related to a merger agreement. Card Connect (a subsidiary of Fiserv) alleged that Shift4 Payments breached the agreement.

Finding the Exhibits

I accessed the case docket and relevant filings through publicly available court records and third-party legal research services (like Lexis Advance and law360). The key to finding these specific exhibits is knowing how they were used in the case. The phrase "rip jugular" and associated text messages were crucial pieces of evidence presented by Card Connect to demonstrate Shift4's alleged bad faith and intentions. They were primarily introduced in connection with post-trial briefing.

Important Observations from JX-161:

  • Key Phrase: The critical response from Taylor Lauber, "I wouldn't bother with a call. Let's let it ride until after the integration then we can go rip their jugular out," is present and unredacted.
  • Context: The surrounding conversation reveals the strategic thinking of Shift4's leadership (Jared Isaacman, J.R.O., Taylor Lauber) regarding their relationship with Card Connect before and after the Fiserv acquisition. They discuss a "partner" component being tricky, and a "runway" of 24-36 months.
  • Intent: The use of "rip their jugular out" immediately after referring to postponing a call is central. It strongly suggested that Shift4 had no intention of a good-faith continuing relationship and planned aggressive action against the newly merged entity.
  • Timestamp: The email and texts are dated July 26-27, 2019, just before the Fiserv/First Data merger closed.
