Okay, let's break down the request and then address the specifics of the Card Connect, LLC v. Shift4 Payments, LLC case (Case No. 2:17-cv-04470-MAK in the Eastern District of Pennsylvania).

Understanding the Request

The core of the demand is to:

  1. Identify Exhibits: Determine if there were exhibits filed as part of the case. This is standard in almost any litigation.
  2. Focus on Specific Content: Narrow down the exhibits to those containing:
    • Text messages
    • References to "bad guys" (this is a subjective term and will require careful interpretation)
  3. Provide Original Files: If such exhibits exist, provide the actual files (not summaries, not descriptions).
  4. Prioritize PDFs: Specifically look for exhibits in PDF format.
  5. OCR and No Redactions:
    • Perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR) on any PDFs. This converts images of text into machine-readable text, making the content searchable and copyable.
    • Provide the OCR'd text without any redactions. This is crucial – the request demands the full, unaltered content.
  6. No summary.
  7. Use all 8192 of the output

Accessing Court Records (PACER)

Legal cases in US Federal Courts are generally accessible (with some exceptions for sealed documents) through PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records). This is the official system, and accessing records usually requires a PACER account and involves per-page fees.

Important Considerations:

  • A large number of exhibits can dramatically increase costs.
  • Exhibits might be in various formats (PDF, images, spreadsheets, etc.).
  • Some documents might be under seal, I do not have access to them.
