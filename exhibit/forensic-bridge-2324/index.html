Case Background:

The text messages show discussions regarding the transition from First Data (Fiserv) to Shift4's own platform. Key points and the "Forensic Bridge" mention:

  • Jared Isaacman's Intent (Feb 8, 2021): Jared Isaacman (Shift4's CEO) expresses a clear desire to completely separate from Fiserv/First Data by the end of the contract term in November. He's even willing to make the remaining earn-out payments early.
  • "Hard Stop" in November (Feb 9, 2021): Brad Herring confirms the plan for a "hard stop" in November, indicating a complete cut-off of the relationship with Fiserv.
  • Initial Bridge Confusion (Feb 10, 2021): Nancy Disman mentions receiving information about clients moving to the "bridge" by April 1st. Brad Herring indicates that they were told there would be no bridge until November, and there's "a lot of noise" around the issue. He plans to get clarity from Fiserv.
  • Forensic Bridge Issue (Feb 23, 2021): Michael Isaacman asks Brad Herring to contact someone at Fiserv ([redacted]'s contact information is provided) who is "dealing with the Forensic Bridge issue." The concern is that Fiserv is being told everything is moving "all at once," and the contact wants to clarify the timeline and what's being moved.
