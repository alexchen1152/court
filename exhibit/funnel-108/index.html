Key Takeaways and Analysis Pertaining to Potential Trade Secret Information

  • High-Level Strategic Discussions: The emails reveal a very high level of strategic partnership discussions between Shift4 and First Data (and its subsidiary CardConnect).   • Repeated Interest in Partnership/Acquisition: Shift4 consistently pursued a "strategic partnership" with First Data. The language used ("big opportunities," "increase annual EBITDA," "unlock additional value") strongly suggests Shift4 was pitching its capabilities as either an acquisition target or a crucial, deeply integrated partner. The discussion with an executive talking about potentially increasing ebitda of first data merchant organization 1% to 10%
  • First Data Asset Optimization: Shift4 specifically mentions that its technology could "give lift" to First Data's acquired assets, including "BluePay, Card Connect, etc." This is a crucial point related to the lawsuit. Shift4 is claiming its technology can improve the performance of CardConnect, the very entity suing them. This statement, made before any alleged misappropriation, is key evidence.
  • Pitching Integrated Payments Platform: Shift4 was actively presenting its "full-stack payment platform for large software companies" as a valuable asset to First Data. This aligns with their claim that they possessed unique technology and expertise in the ISV (Independent Software Vendor) space.
  • CardConnect's Direct Engagement: Prior to the high-level discussions with First Data, Shift4 had been in direct contact with CardConnect (Nate Hirshberg) about a potential partnership. This establishes a prior relationship and context for CardConnect's claims. They were aware of Shift4's capabilities from direct interaction.
  • No "Funnel" Mention (but Implied): While "funnel" isn't mentioned explicitly, several discussions implicitly relate to customer acquisition and portfolio management. Discussions around "portfolio management and revenue tracking tools" (CoPilot) touch on aspects of a sales or business development funnel.

Conclusion in Relation to Trade Secrets

The emails paint a clear picture:

  1. Shift4 possessed and actively marketed a specific set of capabilities, particularly in integrated payments and ISV solutions, before the alleged misappropriation. This supports their defense that their knowledge was pre-existing and not solely derived from CardConnect's information.

  2. CardConnect was aware of these capabilities through direct discussions and presentations.

  3. The strategic discussions with First Data indicate that the highest levels of management considered Shift4's offerings valuable and potentially synergistic with their existing businesses, including CardConnect.

The absence of the specific term "funnel" doesn't negate the strong implications related to customer acquisition, portfolio management, and the ISV market, all of which are relevant to a trade secrets dispute in the payments industry.
