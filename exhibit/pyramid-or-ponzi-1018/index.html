Case Background

This case involved a contract dispute between Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments, LLC. Card Connect alleged that Shift4 breached their agreement, particularly concerning the migration of merchant accounts and related business practices. Shift4 counterclaimed, alleging, among other things, breaches of contract and fraudulent inducement. It went to trial, and it involved complex arguments over payment processing, contractual obligations, and business conduct.

Key Observations and Context

  • "Pyramid/Ponzi" Allegations: The text messages in DX-0690, DX-0691, DX-0692, DX-0693, DX-0700, DX-0701, and DX-0725 directly mention "pyramid scheme" and "ponzi". These messages are between "JIK" (presumably J. Isaacman) and "Jared" (presumably Jared Isaacman), and separately between "Taylor" and "Jared". This indicates an internal discussion at Shift4 about concerns regarding the business practices of an individual (identified as "John"). The context is crucial, and here it is about financial impropriety.
  • Dispute Over Fees and BIN Sponsorship: DX-0149, DX-0181, and DX-0218 center on communications with Fiserv (represented by Taylor and others). These highlight a major point of contention: Fiserv's demand for referral fees on all referred merchants, regardless of activation, and their refusal to provide BIN sponsorship to Shift4 merchants outside of specific arrangements. This is at the heart of, or caused by, the contract dispute.
  • Fiserv's Refusal to Cooperate: The exhibits consistently show Fiserv's unwillingness to assist Shift4 in activating merchants and their stating they would no longer support Shift4, as reflected in both internal Shift4 communications, and messaging to Fiserv, along with their replies.
  • Contractual Concerns: There had been concerns of prior verbal contracts, as pointed out in multiple exhibits.
