Crucial Considerations and Limitations:

  • Public Availability: Court records, including exhibits, are often publicly accessible, but not always. Access depends on the court, the case type, and any sealing orders. Federal cases are typically available through PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records), but exhibits can be large and downloading them all can be time-consuming and costly. Some state courts have online portals, while others require in-person requests.

Getting unredacted versions requires a specific legal process (a motion to unseal) and a compelling reason that outweighs the reasons for sealing. It's highly unlikely you'll get unredacted versions without being a party to the case and having a strong legal basis.
