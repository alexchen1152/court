I, Jared Isaacman, declare as follows:

  1. I am over the age of eighteen, and, if called as a witness, I could and would testify competently to the matters set forth herein based upon my personal knowledge.

  2. I am the Chief Executive Officer of Shift4 Payments, LLC (“Shift4”), which I founded in 1999. I have held that position at all times relevant to this litigation. I have personal knowledge of the facts set forth herein.

  3. Shift4 is a leading provider of integrated payment processing solutions. Shift4’s technology platform, which includes a payment gateway and point of sale (“POS”) software and hardware, provides a single integration point to an end-to-end payments offering and enables a unified commerce experience.

  4. In 2015, Shift4 began marketing its end-to-end payment processing solution, which it branded as “Dollars on the Net.”

  5. On January 20, 2016, I sent an email to Jeffrey Shanahan, the then-CEO of CardConnect, to inquire about a meeting. At the time, Mr. Shanahan had not been named CEO, and the position was held by Brian Peters.

  6. I had several meetings with Mr. Shanahan between January 2016 and February 2017, including via telephone, to discuss a potential business combination between Shift4 and CardConnect.

  7. In fall 2016, I also began having similar discussions with FTV Capital and CardConnect’s CFO, Chuck Bernicker.

  8. These discussions with Mr. Shanahan, FTV Capital, and Mr. Bernicker became serious in early 2017.

  9. On or about March 6, 2017, I sent several text messages to Mr. Shanahan in response to recent news regarding a potential business combination between Vantiv and WorldPay. It appeared that Vantiv was planning to move forward with an acquisition of Aon instead of WorldPay opening up strategic options for WorldPay, including an acqusition of CardConnect. I sent Mr. Shanahan these text messages, stating, "So AON is the target. Which means there is the option to take card connect to worldpay...or us [Shift4]."

  10. A true and correct copy of this text message exchange is attached hereto. The true and correct copies were extracted from Slack, as described below. Case 2:19-cv-01329-JD Document 45-8 Filed 08/13/19 Page 1 of 3 Case 2:19-cv-01329-JD Document 45-8 Filed 08/13/19 Page 2 of 3

  11. I asked Mr. Shanahan via text message, “Should we get creative?” I further stated, "We could structure anything . . . merger w cash out . . . Go private ... Lots of ways so ftv [FTV Capital] and the team win." I also stated, "Our gateway is the best next gen tech out there. It wld [would] give us great scale. But you know that:"

  12. In June 2017, First Data Merchant Services, LLC (“First Data”) acquired CardConnect.

  13. After the acquisition, CardConnect became a sales agent of First Data, with the ability to resell First Data’s payment processing services to CardConnect’s customers.

  14. In light of the acquisition, I had conversations with CardConnect representatives regarding a potential relationship between Shift4 and CardConnect whereby Shift4 (via its gateway) could provide the same services as CardConnect (via its gateway). These discussions concerned Shift4’s potential acquisition of CardConnect assets and customer relationships from First Data. Ultimately, First Data decided not to enter into that transaction.

  15. I also had discussions with First Data representatives about other strategic opportunities involving CardConnect, including potentially selling First Data some or all of Shift4. These discussions did not result in an agreement either.

  16. In connection with these various discussions regarding transactions between Shift4, CardConnect, and First Data, I understood that CardConnect, as First Data’s sales agent, had an existing referral agreement with First Data (the “Referral Agreement”) and that such Referral Agreement would need to be addressed in connection with any transaction between Shift4, First Data, and CardConnect.

  17. Sometime shortly after the acquisition, I had discussions with CardConnect representatives about the potential use of Shift4’s gateway with CardConnect customers. These conversations included discussion of a potential “special arrangement” between Shift4 and CardConnect. However, no agreement was reached.

  18. The various transaction-related conversations that I had with CardConnect and First Data representatives involved a variety of transaction structures, including Shift4 acquiring CardConnect customer relationships from First Data and/or merging the two companies. These discussions ultimately did not result in any transaction and were discontinued by early 2018. I specifically recall informing Mr. Shanahan by phone in early 2018 that an acquisition of Shift4 was not something my executive team and I were interested in pursuing at the time.

  19. In connection with at least one of our conversations about a possible transaction at that time, I suggested to Mr. Bernicker that the parties avoid putting anything in writing until reaching verbal agreement, and I never saw a draft of any written agreement reflecting the transaction terms of my discussions with Mr. Bernicker and other CardConnect representatives.

  20. In sum, from the time of the CardConnect acquisition to early 2018, my discussions with CardConnect and First Data evolved from me reaching out to Mr. Shanahan to see what opportunities were available to several months of fast-moving but ultimately unsuccessful discussions regarding a potential transaction among the parties.

  21. In March 2018, Shift4 hired Jonathan Maurillo as its Senior Vice President of Strategic Partnerships.

  22. I had conversations with Mr. Maurillo about CardConnect, in which I told Mr. Maurillo that although there had been high level transaction-related conversations between Shift4 and both CardConnect and First Data in the past, those conversations were discontinued earlier that year.

    I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the United States of America that the foregoing is true and correct.

    Executed on August 13, 2019.

Jared Isaacman

Document 45-9

Exhibit H, Declaration of Jonathan Maurillo

EXHIBIT H

I, Jonathan Maurillo, declare as follows:

  1. I am over the age of eighteen and, if called as a witness, I could and would testify competently to the matters set forth below based on my own personal knowledge, information, and belief.

  2. I am the Senior Vice President of Strategic Partnerships at Shift4 Payments, LLC (“Shift4”). I have held this position since March 2018. I am familiar with the various reseller programs at Shift4 and have personal knowledge of the facts set forth herein.

  3. Shift4 is a leading provider of integrated payment processing solutions.

  4. Shift4’s technology platform, which includes a payment gateway and point of sale (“POS”) software and hardware, provides a single integration point to an end-to-end payments offering and enables a unified commerce experience.

  5. Shift4 provides its services to merchants through a variety of different channels and programs, including through Independent Sales Organizations (“ISOs”) and other reseller partners that leverage Shift4’s payment gateway to provide services.

  6. In my role as Senior Vice President of Strategic Partnerships at Shift4, I am responsible for building and maintaining relationships with Shift4’s ISOs and reseller partners, as well as with POS software and hardware providers.

  7. Prior to joining Shift4, I worked at CardConnect, LLC (“CardConnect”), which was acquired by First Data Merchant Services, LLC (“First Data”) in June 2017. I worked at CardConnect between May 2016 and October 2017.

  8. At CardConnect, I was a Strategic Partner Manager, responsible for identifying and developing strategic partnerships with ISOs and other reseller partners.

  9. After the acquisition, CardConnect became a sales agent of First Data, with the ability to resell First Data’s payment processing services to CardConnect’s customers.

  10. During my time at CardConnect, I learned about the various components of CardConnect’s business. For example, I learned that CardConnect maintained relationships with various ISOs and reseller partners, and that the CardConnect gateway was integrated with various POS software and hardware providers.

  11. I also learned that CardConnect, as a sales agent of First Data, had an existing referral agreement with First Data (the “Referral Agreement”) and that, pursuant to the Referral Agreement, First Data owned the merchant relationships of the ISOs and other reseller partners that used the CardConnect gateway.

  12. After joining Shift4 in March 2018, I had conversations with Shift4 executives, including Shift4’s Chief Executive Officer, Jared Isaacman, about CardConnect.

  13. I was informed by Mr. Isaacman that there had been transaction-related conversations between Shift4 and both CardConnect and First Data before I joined Shift4 in March 2018.

  14. Based on my personal understanding, those transaction-related conversations centered on a potential acquisition of CardConnect customer relationships and assets from First Data, in light of the fact that First Data acquired CardConnect in June 2017. However, First Data decided not to enter into that transaction.

  15. It was also my understanding that there had been conversations regarding whether First Data would acquire some or all of Shift4’s equity. No transaction ever took place between CardConnect and Shift4, or between First Data and Shift4.

  16. I understood that there was a “cool down” period during which Shift4 personnel, including me, were not going to be reaching out to CardConnect regarding any agreements.

  17. Because of my familiarity with CardConnect’s business, I was asked by Shift4 executives to reach out to contacts at CardConnect to discuss a potential “special arrangement” between CardConnect and Shift4, whereby Shift4 (via its gateway) could provide the same services as CardConnect (via its gateway).

  18. I subsequently had those conversations with CardConnect executives and presented the proposal that was authorized by Shift4. Those discussions, however, ultimately did not result in any agreement.

  19. I declare under penalty of perjury, under the laws of the United States of America, that the foregoing is true and correct to the best of my knowledge, information, and belief.
