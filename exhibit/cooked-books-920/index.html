Case Background & Context

This case involved a contractual dispute arising from the acquisition of Shift4 Payments, LLC (and its subsidiaries, including Merchant-Link) by Card Connect, LLC (later acquired by Fiserv). A key element of the dispute centered around earn-out payments based on Shift4's financial performance after the acquisition. Fiserv/CardConnect alleged that Shift4 manipulated its accounting and engaged in fraudulent practices to inflate its performance and trigger larger earn-out payments.

  1. Exhibit 1: "Sham" contract and the text messages.

    • Court's Description: This exhibit focuses on a contract between Shift4 and a customer (LVI) that Fiserv alleged was a "sham" designed to inflate Shift4's revenue. There are text messsages within that discussion.

    • Relevant Text Messages (quoted portions found in the court opinion):

      • J.D. Oder (Shift4 CEO) to Taylor Lauber (Shift4): "We just need the LVI contract signed and we're done for the year."
      • Oder to Lauber: "Put a scope of work on the contract and I think we can get 400k of it booked this month."
      • Oder to Lauber: "Tell them if they sign one, we will give them something of significant value before year-end. We just can't tell them that yet."
      • Lauber to LVI: "Would be a huge help in 2019, we can make it worth it."

    • "Cooked Books" Implication: The court opinion highlights that Fiserv alleged this contract was backdated, had a vague scope of work, and LVI never intended to pay the full amount. This ties into the "cooked books" allegation because it suggests intentional misrepresentation of revenue.

  2. Exhibit 2: Text Messages re: Revenue Recognition.

    • Court's Description: This references texts about when new customer contracts should be recognized as revenue.

    • Relevant Text Messages (quoted portions):

      • Oder to an employee: "Can we book any of the revenue on these now? Or literally not until they do a transaction."
      • Oder requesting immediate invoicing of a customer "even though the customer was not yet live."

    • "Cooked Books" Implication: This suggests pushing revenue recognition earlier than GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) would allow, artificially inflating the financials for the earn-out period.
