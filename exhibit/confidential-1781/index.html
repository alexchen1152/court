Case Background (for context, not part of the exhibits):

  • This case involved a contractual dispute between Card Connect, LLC (a subsidiary of Fiserv) and Shift4 Payments, LLC. The core issue was a reseller agreement and alleged breaches of that agreement, including non-payment of residuals and solicitation of merchants.

Key Observations and Summary:

  • Payment Disputes: The core of the email exchanges revolves around significant outstanding balances owed by Shift4 to CardConnect (Fiserv). These include both merchant billing fees and fees related to the IAPS (Integrated Application Processing System) program.
  • IAPS Service Issues: Shift4 repeatedly expresses dissatisfaction with the IAPS service provided by CardConnect, citing a lack of consistent data and support. This is used as justification for withholding payment.
  • Threats of Termination: CardConnect (Fiserv) repeatedly threatens to terminate services to Shift4's merchants due to non-payment. This creates a sense of urgency in the communications.
  • Accounting Discrepancies: There's a clear indication of ongoing accounting and reconciliation issues, with Shift4 disputing the amounts owed and citing errors in CardConnect's billing.
  • Internal Discussions: The emails reveal internal discussions within Shift4, primarily between Jared Isaacman (CEO), J.D. Oder II, and Taylor Lauber, regarding how to handle the payment demands and service issues.
  • Legal mentions, there is an escalation to Fiserv's legal team on more than one instance within these documents.
